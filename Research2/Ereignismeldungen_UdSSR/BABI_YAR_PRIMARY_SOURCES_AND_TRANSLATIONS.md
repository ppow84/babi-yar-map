# Babi Yar / Babyn Yar — Primary Source Documents & Translations
## September–October 1941 | Kiev (Kiew), Ukraine
### Compiled from Bundesarchiv R 58/218, NARA, USHMM, NS-Archiv, and Wikimedia/Ukrainian State Archives

## PART I: GERMAN OPERATIONAL REPORTS (EREIGNISMELDUNGEN UDSSR)

### Document 1 — Ereignismeldung UdSSR Nr. 101  
**Date:** Berlin, 2. Oktober 1941  
**Archive:** Bundesarchiv R 58/218, fol. 2–3  
**Scan files:** `EM101_cover_7Okt1941.jpg`, `EM101_p3_Kiew_33771_2Okt1941.jpg`  
**Classification:** GEHEIME REICHSSACHE (Secret Reich Matter)  
**Issued by:** Chef der Sicherheitspolizei und des SD  
**Copies made:** 48 Ausfertigungen (48 copies)

This is the **first official report** of the Babi Yar massacre, filed just two days after the killings. The relevant passage (folio 3):

#### Original German (Einsatzgruppe C section):
> **Einsatzgruppe C.**  
> **Standort Kiew.**  
> Das Sonderkommando 4a hat in Zusammenarbeit mit Gruppenstab und zwei Kommandos des Polizei-Regiments Süd am 29. und 30.9.41 in Kiew **33.771 Juden exekutiert.**

#### Translation:
> **Einsatzgruppe C.**  
> **Location: Kiev.**  
> Sonderkommando 4a, in cooperation with the group headquarters and two detachments of Police Regiment South, on September 29 and 30, 1941, in Kiev **executed 33,771 Jews.**

### Document 2 — Ereignismeldung UdSSR Nr. 106  
**Date:** Berlin, 7. Oktober 1941  
**Archive:** Bundesarchiv R 58/218, fol. 52–77  
**Scan files:** `EM106_cover_7Okt1941.jpg` through `EM106_p21_Kiew.jpg`  
**Classification:** GEHEIME REICHSSACHE  
**Issued by:** Chef der Sicherheitspolizei und des SD  
**Distribution:** 48 copies (copy 40 of 48)  
**Reference:** R.Nr. IV A 1 – 1 B/41 – gRs –  
**NARA reference:** Folder E 327, submitted for Nuremberg Trials 4.4.1947

This report provides the **detailed account** of the circumstances and conduct of the Babi Yar massacre. The Kiev section spans folios 62–77 (approximately 15 pages).

#### Section on the Kiev fires (folio 62–63, scan pages 127–129):

**Original:**
> Gleich am ersten Tage der Besetzung Kiews wurden seitens der Bevölkerung in grossem Masse Telefonate über Häuser- und andere Sprengmittel in öffentlichen Gebäuden und Obdächern gemacht. Am 20.9.41 gelangte durch Zeitzünder eine Mine in der Zitadelle, in der ein Artilleriedepot untergebracht war, zur Explosion. Hierbei ist u.a. General der Artillerie von Seydlitz gefallen. Am 4.10.41 erfolgte eine Explosion in dem Haus des deutschen Stadtkommandant...

**Translation:**
> From the very first day of Kiev's occupation, the population reported in large numbers by telephone about explosive devices planted in public buildings and rooftops. On September 20, 1941, a mine with a time fuse detonated in the citadel, where an artillery depot was housed. General of Artillery von Seydlitz was among those killed. On October 4, an explosion occurred in the house of the German city commandant...

#### Section on the massacre itself (folio 63–66, scan pages 131–135):

**Original:**
> ...erüchlich gross. Hinzu kommt, dass Juden sich nachweislich an den Brandtaten beteiligt hatten. Die Bevölkerung forderte danach von den zuständigen Behörden entsprechende Vergeltungsmaßnahmen. Aus diesem Grunde wurden im Vereinbarung mit dem Sonderkommandanten sämtliche Juden Kiews aufgefordert, sich am Montag, den 29.9. bis 8.00 Uhr in einem bestimmten Stelle einzufinden. Allein Juden wurden durch die Angehörigen der aufgestellten ukrainischen Miliz in der ganzen Stadt angeschleppt. Gleichzeitig wurde amtlich bekanntgegeben, dass sämtliche Juden Kiews so ausgesiedelt werden.  
>  
> Im Zusammenarbeit mit dem Gruppenstab und 2 Kommandos des Polizei-Regiments Süd hat das Sonderkommando 4a am 29. und 30.9. **33.771 Juden exekutiert.** Geld, Gertsachen, Wäsche und Kleidungsstücke wurden sichergestellt und zum Teil der BdS zur Ausrüstung der Volksdeutschen, zum Teil den beabsichtigten städtischen Verwaltungsorganen zu bedürftigen Bevölkerung übergeben.  
>  
> Die Aktion selbst hat **reibungslos verlaufen.** Irgendhöhe Frischstellen haben sich nicht ergeben.

**Translation:**
> ...considerable in scale. Moreover, Jews were demonstrably involved in the arson attacks. The population therefore demanded corresponding retaliatory measures from the competent authorities. For this reason, in agreement with the city commandant, all Jews of Kiev were ordered to appear by 8:00 a.m. on Monday, September 29, at a specified location. Jews were also brought in from across the entire city by members of the Ukrainian militia that had been established. At the same time, it was officially announced that all Jews of Kiev would be "resettled" in this manner.  
>  
> In cooperation with the group headquarters and 2 detachments of Police Regiment South, Sonderkommando 4a on September 29 and 30 **executed 33,771 Jews.** Money, valuables, linen, and clothing were confiscated and distributed in part to the BdS [Commander of the Security Police] for equipping the ethnic Germans, and in part to the intended municipal administration bodies for the needy population.  
>  
> The action itself **proceeded smoothly.** There were no disruptions of any kind.

#### Aftermath section (folio 67–68, scan pages 137–139):

**Original:**
> Die Sonderstab-Krimmer hatten durch die vier Kiew seitens der Sommer-Komm erfolgten Bestandnahme zu ebensolcher verfäller und würde eine Beschädigung diesem über an Verhältnis von Flugblättern und schießen von Flaksen festgestellt...  
>  
> Am 19.9.41 wurde das Judenviertel ab 6.00 Uhr früh geräumt, nachdem es am Abend wurden von 50 tausend ukrainischen Miliz angesallt und abgesperrt worden war. Der Abtransport erfolgte in 10 Lkw...

**Translation:**
> ...On September 19, 1941, the Jewish quarter was cleared from 6:00 a.m., after it had been surrounded the previous evening by 50,000 Ukrainian militia members and sealed off. The transport was carried out in 10 trucks...

#### Zhitomir section (folio 69, scan page 141):

**Original:**
> ...getroffen worden waren, wurden insgesamt **3 145 Juden registriert und exekutiert.**  
> Den Beauftragten der BdV in Shitomiv, Posen, Schuhbetonten von 29.–30.9.41 an Sachen, Bekleidung, Schuhwerk, Geschirr usw., welche bei dem frühem beschlagnahmt worden waren, zum Abtransport empfehlen werden. Sachlagerhätte Objekt-Stände und Geld wurden den Sonderkommando 4a übergeführt.

**Translation:**
> ...altogether **3,145 Jews were registered and executed.**  
> Items confiscated — clothing, footwear, utensils, etc. — were recommended for transport. Items held in storage and money were transferred to Sonderkommando 4a.

### Document 3 — Ereignismeldung UdSSR Nr. 128  
**Date:** Berlin, 3. November 1941  
**Archive:** Bundesarchiv R 58/218  
**Classification:** GEHEIME REICHSSACHE  
**Issued by:** Chef der Sicherheitspolizei und des SD  
**Distribution:** 50 copies  
**English translation source:** Jewish Virtual Library / Arad, Krakowski & Spector, *The Einsatzgruppen Reports*, pp. 217–220  
**Arolsen Archives scans:** https://collections.arolsen-archives.org/de/archive/1-2-7-20_2273000/

This is the **cumulative summary report** for Einsatzgruppe C, filed five weeks after Babi Yar. It provides the broader context for the massacre and reveals the total scale of killings.

#### Key passages (Einsatzgruppe C — Kiev section):

> "Approximately 80,000 persons have been liquidated by now by the Kommandos of the Einsatzgruppe. Of these, roughly 8,000 were convicted after investigation; the remainder were killed in retaliatory actions."

On the Babi Yar operation specifically:

> "The largest of these actions took place immediately after the occupation of Kiev; it was directed exclusively against Jews and their families. The difficulty in organizing such a large-scale action — above all from the standpoint of registration — was overcome in Kiev by means of posters requesting all Jews to report for resettlement. Although initially only the participation of 5,000 to 6,000 Jews had been expected, more than 30,000 Jews arrived, who, thanks to extremely clever organization, still believed in their resettlement until the very moment of execution."

> "Approximately 75,000 Jews have been liquidated in this manner."

#### Significance:

EM 128 is critical because it:
1. Gives the **cumulative total** of Einsatzgruppe C killings (approx. 80,000) by early November 1941
2. Reveals the **deception strategy** — the resettlement ruse and poster system that lured Jews to Babi Yar
3. Acknowledges the **scale exceeded expectations** — over 30,000 arrived when only 5–6,000 were anticipated
4. Notes the ongoing challenge that "the number of Jews still found in the city always surpasses considerably the number of executed Jews" — indicating the killings continued after the main September 29–30 action
5. Distinguishes between those "convicted after investigation" (~8,000) and the vast majority killed "in retaliatory actions" — a euphemism for mass execution without any individual process

This report was extensively quoted in the Rüß article (MGM 1998) and used at the Nuremberg Einsatzgruppen Trial (Case No. 9).

## PART II: THE BEKANNTMACHUNG (NOTIFICATION POSTER)

**Date:** September 28, 1941  
**Issued by:** German occupation authorities / Sonderkommando 4a  
**Languages:** German, Russian, Ukrainian  
**Scan file:** `Bekanntmachung_Kiev_28Sep1941_Wikimedia.jpg`  
**Source:** Ukrainian State Archives (archives.gov.ua) via Wikimedia Commons  
**Copies posted:** Approximately 2,000 throughout Kiev

#### German text (bottom portion of poster):

> Sämtliche Juden der Stadt Kiew und Umgebung haben sich am Montag, dem 29. September 1941, um 8 Uhr, Ecke der Melnik- und Dokterivski-Straße (an den Friedhöfen) einzufinden. Zu bringen: Dokumente, Geld und Wertsachen sowie warme Kleidung, Wäsche usw.  
> Wer von den Juden dieser Anordnung nicht Folge leistet und anderswo angetroffen wird, **wird erschossen.**  
> Wer von den Bürgern in die verlassenen Wohnungen der Juden eindringt und die darin befindlichen Sachen sich aneignet, **wird erschossen.**

#### Translation:
> All Jews of the city of Kiev and its surrounding area are to appear on Monday, September 29, 1941, at 8 o'clock, at the corner of Melnik and Doktorivska Streets (near the cemeteries). Bring: documents, money and valuables, as well as warm clothing, linen, etc.  
> Any Jew who fails to comply with this order and is found elsewhere **will be shot.**  
> Any citizen who enters the apartments left behind by Jews and appropriates the items in them **will be shot.**

**Note:** The notice gave no indication that those assembling faced death. The instruction to bring valuables, documents, and warm clothing was designed to disguise the action as a "resettlement."

## PART III: THE VON REICHENAU ORDER — FOLLOW-ON POLICY DIRECTIVE

**Title:** Betr.: Verhalten der Truppe im Ostraum (Re: Conduct of Troops in the Eastern Theater)  
**Date:** A.H.Qu., 10. Oktober 1941  
**Issued by:** Armee-Oberkommando 6, Abt. 1a — signed: v. Reichenau, Generalfeldmarschall  
**Distributed by:** Oberkommando der Heeresgruppe Süd, Ia Nr. 2682/41 geh., 12.10.1941  
**Distributed to:** AOK 11, AOK 17, Pz.AOK 1, Befh.rückw.H.Geb. Süd, Mil.Befh. Ukraine, AOK 6  
**Source:** NS-Archiv (ns-archiv.de); originally published in: John Mendelson (ed.), *The Holocaust: Selected Documents*, Vol. 10, New York/London 1982, pp. 7ff.

**Note:** This order was issued just 10 days after the Babi Yar massacre and 3 days after Ereignismeldung Nr. 106. It was endorsed by Field Marshal von Rundstedt and distributed to all army groups on the Eastern Front. Hitler reportedly praised the order as exemplary.

#### Full German text:

> **Betr.: Verhalten der Truppe im Ostraum.**
>
> Hinsichtlich des Verhaltens der Truppe gegenüber dem bolschewistischen System bestehen vielfach noch unklare Vorstellungen. Das wesentlichste Ziel des Feldzuges gegen das jüdischbolschewistische System ist die völlige Zerschlagung der Machtmittel und die **Ausrottung des asiatischen Einflusses im europäischen Kulturkreis.**
>
> Hierdurch entstehen auch für die Truppe Aufgaben, die über das hergebrachte einseitige Soldatentum hinausgehen. Der Soldat ist im Ostraum nicht nur ein Kämpfer nach den Regeln der Kriegskunst, sondern auch Träger einer unerbittlichen völkischen Idee und der Rächer für alle Bestialitäten, die deutschem und artverwandtem Volkstum zugefügt wurden.
>
> Deshalb muß der Soldat für die Notwendigkeit der harten, aber gerechten Sühne am **jüdischen Untermenschentum** volles Verständnis haben. Sie hat den weiteren Zweck, Erhebungen im Rücken der Wehrmacht, die erfahrungsgemäß stets von Juden angezettelt wurden, im Keime zu ersticken.
>
> Der Kampf gegen den Feind hinter der Front wird noch nicht ernst genug genommen. Immer noch werden heimtückische grausame Partisanen und entartete Weiber zu Kriegsgefangenen gemacht...
>
> [Middle sections on supplies, building fires, disarmament of population]
>
> Fern von allen politischen Erwägungen der Zukunft hat der Soldat zweierlei zu erfüllen:  
> 1) die völlige Vernichtung der bolschewistischen Irrlehre, des Sowjet-Staates und seiner Wehrmacht;  
> 2) die erbarmungslose Ausrottung artfremder Heimtücke und Grausamkeit und damit die Sicherung des Lebens der deutschen Wehrmacht in Rußland.
>
> Nur so werden wir unserer geschichtlichen Aufgabe gerecht, **das deutsche Volk von der asiatisch-jüdischen Gefahr ein für allemal zu befreien.**
>
> Der Oberbefehlshaber: gez. v. Reichenau, Generalfeldmarschall

#### Translation (key passages):

> **Re: Conduct of Troops in the Eastern Theater.**
>
> Regarding the behavior of the troops toward the Bolshevist system, there are still many unclear ideas. The most essential goal of the campaign against the Jewish-Bolshevist system is the complete destruction of its means of power and the **extermination of Asiatic influence in the European cultural sphere.**
>
> This creates for the troops tasks that go beyond the traditional one-sided role of the soldier. The soldier in the Eastern Theater is not merely a fighter according to the rules of the art of war, but also the bearer of a relentless national idea and the avenger of all the bestialities inflicted upon the German and related peoples.
>
> Therefore the soldier must have full understanding of the necessity of the harsh but just atonement being demanded of **Jewish sub-humanity.** This has the further purpose of nipping in the bud any uprisings in the rear of the Wehrmacht, which according to experience are invariably instigated by Jews.
>
> Far from all political considerations of the future, the soldier has two things to accomplish:  
> 1) the complete annihilation of the Bolshevist heresy, the Soviet State and its armed forces;  
> 2) the merciless extermination of alien cunning and cruelty and thereby the protection of the lives of the German Wehrmacht in Russia.
>
> Only in this way shall we fulfill our historical duty to **liberate the German people once and for all from the Asiatic-Jewish danger.**

**Significance:** This order, issued just 10 days after Babi Yar, made explicit to all Wehrmacht units that the mass killing of Jews was to be understood as integral to the war effort — not merely tolerated but actively embraced as "historical duty." It was subsequently adapted by Generals Hoth (Nov. 17) and von Manstein (Nov. 20, 1941) in similarly worded orders.

## PART IV: CORROBORATING EYEWITNESS ACCOUNTS FROM KIEV RESIDENTS

### 4.1 — Iryna Khoroshunova (Kiev resident, engineer/draftsperson)

**Diary entries, September–October 1941**  
**Language:** Russian (written in occupied Kiev)  
**Archive:** USHMM RG-31.056.0001; EHRI training collection (PDF scan + English translation)  
**Published in:** *Die Schoáh von Babij Jar* (Konstanz: Hartung-Gorre, 1991), pp. 263–328  
**URL:** https://collections.ushmm.org/search/catalog/irn524104  
**Note:** Multiple versions survive (at least 4 in various Kyiv museums and archives). The EHRI PDF was OCR'd for this document; original is in Russian Cyrillic.

**September 30, 1941** (day after the massacre):

*Russian original (from OCR of archival scan):*
> «Мы всё ещё не знаем, что сталось с евреями. Страшное. Доносится с Лукьяновского кладбища... Говорят, что евреев расстреляли. Те, кто провожал их до пункта сбора, [говорят что им] приказано было двигаться назад. Вся дорога запружена евреями, окружёнными немецкими солдатами. Немецкие солдаты бросали их вещи, а их самих тогда...»

*Translation:*
> "We still do not know what has become of the Jews. Terrible. [Gunfire] can be heard from the direction of Lukyanivske cemetery... They say the Jews have been shot. Those who accompanied them to the assembly point say they were ordered to turn back. The entire road was packed with Jews, surrounded by German soldiers. The German soldiers threw their belongings aside, and they themselves then..."

**October 2, 1941** (same day Ereignismeldung Nr. 101 was filed in Berlin):

*Russian original:*
> «Уже все говорят, что евреев убивают. Нет, не убивают — а уже убили. Всех, без разбора — стариков, женщин, детей. Так говорят, но сомнений быть не может. Говорят, что евреев расстреляли из пулемётов. Другие говорят, что их живыми закопали. В понедельник вечером или ночью расстреляли уже тогда.»  
>  
> «Одна русская женщина проводила на кладбище свою подругу, и сама через забор просочилась с другой стороны. Она видела, как раздетых людей вели в сторону Бабьего Яра, и слышала стрельбу.»  
>  
> «Расстрел евреев — факт. Сам этот факт, от которого мы все начинаем сходить с ума. Жить с сознанием этого факта невозможно. Слёзы вокруг нас. А мы? Мы тоже плакали 29 сентября, когда думали, что их везут в концлагерь. А теперь? Разве возможно плакать?»

*Translation:*
> "Now everyone is saying that the Jews are being killed. No — not being killed — **they are already dead.** All of them, without distinction: old people, women, children. So they say, and there can be no doubt. They say the Jews were shot with machine guns. Others say they were buried alive. On Monday evening or night they were shot at that point."  
>  
> "One Russian woman accompanied her [Jewish] girlfriend to the cemetery, then squeezed through a fence to the other side. **She saw how naked people were being led in the direction of Babi Yar, and heard the shooting.**"  
>  
> "The shooting of the Jews is a fact. It is this fact from which we are all beginning to lose our minds. It is impossible to live with the awareness of this fact. Those around us are weeping. And we? We also wept on September 29th, when we thought they were being taken to a concentration camp. And now? Is it even possible to weep?"

### 4.3 — Dmitri Orlov (Kiev resident)

**Testimony, 1945–1946**  
**Source:** Soviet Extraordinary Commission records; reproduced in *Babi Yar: Ravine of Oblivion*

> "Crowds of people [were] walking in an uninterrupted stream along Lvov Street while German patrols lined the pavements. So many people were moving from early morning until late at night that it was difficult to cross the street, and this lasted for three days and three nights."

### 4.4 — Dina Mironovna Pronicheva (Babi Yar survivor)

**Testimony given:** January 24, 1946 (Kiev war crimes trial); April 24, 1946 (Soviet historians)  
**Archive:** TsDAHO fond 166, opys 3, sprava 245; USHMM pa15588  
**Filmed by:** Soviet Ukrainian newsreel

Pronicheva was a Jewish actress at the Kiev Puppet Theatre. Her parents and sister were killed at Babi Yar. She is the only known survivor to have testified at a war crimes trial.

> "The Jews by the thousands were on the way... Every few minutes a group of men, women and children were ordered to disrobe and to stand on the edge of a long ravine and... then they were killed by machine guns."

> "A young naked woman feeding a naked baby with her breast, when a Ukrainian policeman grabbed the infant and threw it into the ravine."

After being ordered to undress, Pronicheva jumped into the pit. She survived by remaining motionless among the dead bodies and later escaped under cover of darkness.

### 4.5 — Anonymous POW Account

**Source:** Soviet Extraordinary Commission records

Soviet prisoners of war held in a camp near Kiev from late September 1941 onward reported witnessing: Jewish prisoners who were below age 16 or above 35 were loaded onto trucks. The trucks returned empty but with the prisoners' clothing left behind, making clear to the remaining prisoners that those taken had been shot.

### 4.6 — Fritz Hofer (German truck driver, Wehrmacht)

**Testimony given:** Post-war trial proceedings  
**Role:** Wehrmacht truck driver ordered to collect confiscated clothing  
**Source:** World Holocaust Forum Foundation; reproduced in multiple trial records  
**URL:** https://worldholocaustforum.org/the-holocaust-in-faces/from-the-testimony-of-hefer-a-german-truck-driver-on-the-extermination-of-jews-at-babi-yar-in-september-29-30-1941/

Hofer (also spelled Hefer/Höfer) was ordered to drive trucks to Babi Yar on September 29–30 to collect the clothing of those murdered. He described passing columns of Jews marching toward the ravine at approximately 10 a.m. He witnessed the undressing procedure and estimated victims stripped in "less than a minute from the moment a person took off his coat." He described the ravine as approximately **150 metres long, 30 metres wide, and 15 metres deep**, with corpses arranged in rows. He estimated two shooters with machine guns were executing victims in the pit.

His testimony is significant as an uncoerced German participant account confirming the scale and method of the killing.

---

### 4.7 — Kurt Werner (Sonderkommando 4a member)

**Role:** Direct perpetrator — went into the ravine and participated in shootings  
**Date of events:** September 29, 1941  
**Source:** Post-war West German judicial proceedings; reproduced in Karel Berkhoff, *Harvest of Despair* (2004); Radio Svoboda/Current Time archive  

Werner testified that the entire Kommando set out at approximately 6 a.m. on September 29, driving north for about 20 minutes on a cobbled road. He described the ravine he was sent into as:

> "approximately 10 metres deep, 400 metres long, 80 metres wide across the top, and 10 metres wide at the bottom."

He stated that he and other Sonderkommando members were sent down into the ravine to carry out executions. Shooting continued until approximately 17:00–18:00 on September 29. His testimony provides the most precise physical description of the site from a perpetrator who was inside the ravine during the killings.

---

### 4.8 — Zakhar Trubakov (Jewish survivor, Kiev)

**Role:** Jewish Kyiv resident who turned back before reaching the shooting site  
**Published account:** *The Riddle of Babi Yar: The True Story Told by a Survivor* (2015)  
**Source:** Amazon/publisher record; Holocaust survivor memoirs

Trubakov was among the Jews assembled on September 29. He heard gunshots in the direction of Babi Yar and, realizing what they meant, turned back and survived. He later became a forced laborer in August 1943 when the Germans used Jewish prisoners to exhume and burn the corpses to destroy evidence of the massacre. His account is doubly valuable: as a witness to the assembly and march on September 29, and as a witness to the 1943 cover-up operation (Sonderaktion 1005).

---

### 4.9 — Nikolai Popov and Aleksei Popov (Soviet citizens, Kiev)

**Testimonies written:** 1941 and December 4, 1941 respectively, commissioned by the NKVD  
**Archive:** Central State Archive of Civic Organizations of Ukraine (TsDAHOU)  
**Published in:** Karel Berkhoff, "'The Corpses in the Ravine Were Women, Men, and Children': Written Testimonies from 1941 on the Babi Yar Massacre," *Holocaust and Genocide Studies*, Vol. 29, No. 2 (2015), pp. 251–285. DOI: 10.1093/hgs/dcv028  

These are the **earliest documented written testimonies** about Babi Yar, composed in the autumn and winter of 1941 by Soviet civilians at NKVD request. According to Berkhoff's analysis, they contain "details not found elsewhere" about the massacre. The NKVD later used these documents as the basis for official reports, introducing edits, cuts, and additions, making the original raw testimonies (still in the TsDAHOU archive) particularly valuable for establishing what was known and when.

---

### 4.10 — Ioanna Ievhenieva (Ukrainian girl, age 13)

**Location during massacre:** Red Army canteen approximately 70 metres from the ravine  
**Source:** Karel Berkhoff, *Babi Yar: Site of Mass Murder, Ravine of Oblivion*, USHMM Occasional Paper 2011-02. URL: https://www.ushmm.org/m/pdfs/Publication_OP_2011-02.pdf

Ievhenieva secretly observed the killings from the canteen. She described seeing "a beautiful girl with curly black hair" who refused to undress and was beaten severely. Her account, cited by Berkhoff, is one of the very few testimonies from a non-Jewish civilian who was physically close enough to observe the actual undressing and killing process.

## PART V: AXIS PRESS COVERAGE

### 5.1 — Felice Bellotti, *La Stampa* (Turin), 31 October 1941

**Headline:** "Le mine 'assassine' di Kiev" (*The 'Assassin' Mines of Kiev*)  
**Subheadline:** "Quando la guerra non è più questione d'onore" (*When war is no longer a matter of honor*)  
**Author:** Felice Bellotti, La Stampa war correspondent  
**Date:** 31 October 1941, p. 3  
**Archive:** Archivio Storico La Stampa; digitized by EHRI as Document B05  
**EHRI:** https://training.ehri-project.eu/b05-la-stampa-article-31th-october-1941  
**Scholarly citation:** Karel Berkhoff, "Babi Yar: Site of Mass Murder, Ravine of Oblivion," USHMM Occasional Paper 2011-02, pp. 22–23, fn. 15; also cited in Ievhen Onats'kyi, "Ukraina ochyma italiis'kykh korespondentiv u druhii svitovii viini," *Samostiina Ukraina* 18, no. 2 (New York, 1965), p. 21.

**Background:** In October 1941, German occupation authorities organized a propaganda press junket, transporting **thirty foreign correspondents from ten countries** by bus from Berlin to Kyiv. Bellotti was La Stampa's delegate.

**Key content:** During the tour, a representative of the German city administration made the following statement to the assembled journalists:

> *"There used to be 350,000 Jews in the city; but today, there were no more Jews."*

Bellotti reported this statement and **explicitly asked in print where the Jews had gone** — "dove sono gli ebrei?" This question, published in a Fascist newspaper in an Axis country, one month after Babi Yar, is one of the earliest instances of any Western journalist acknowledging the total disappearance of Kiev's Jewish population.

As Berkhoff writes: *"An Italian newspaper reported this statement, and wondered where the Jews had gone. Other reports of the visit, such as the one in the Chicago Daily Tribune, omitted this conversation."*

The *Chicago Daily Tribune* correspondent Alex Small filed on the same junket ("Ruins of 5 Day Blaze — Kiev's War Reminder," October 23, 1941, p. 1) and said nothing about the Jews' absence. Bellotti noticed, and said something.

**Significance:** A German official was openly boasting to foreign journalists that 350,000 Jews had ceased to exist in Kyiv. An Italian Fascist reporter printed the statement and asked the question no one in power wanted answered. The article's headline — "The Assassin Mines of Kiev" — gave it cover as a story about Soviet sabotage; but embedded within it was the clearest available acknowledgment in the contemporary Western press that the Jews of Kyiv were gone.

### 5.2 — Ukrainian Nationalist Press (Polissia Sich), 1941–1942

**Article:** "Інтерв'ю з 'Міжкою'" (*Interview with 'Mizhka'*) — Interview with a 15-year-old insurgent from the Поліська Січ (Polissia Sich)  
**Publication:** Ukrainian-language partisan newspaper, likely published in Volhynia/Polissia region  
**Photo caption:** "Начальник Окружної Міліції у Сарнах і Командант партизанських відділів 'Поліська Січ' Тарас Боровець зі своїм штабом" (*Chief of Regional Police in Sarny and Commander of Polissia Sich partisan units, Taras Borovets with his staff*)

The Polissia Sich (commanded by Taras Borovets/Bulba-Borovets) was a Ukrainian nationalist partisan force operating in Polissia/Volhynia during 1941–42, initially fighting Soviet forces. This interview with a 15-year-old fighter named Mykhailo ("Mizhka") Pavlenko illustrates the contemporary Ukrainian nationalist milieu. The article contains casual antisemitic language and a passage in which the boy describes killing a Jewish soldier, and references his sister having "gone with the Jews" (fled with retreating Soviet forces). The Sich aimed to eventually reach Kyiv ("до Києва дійти").

**Significance:** Documents the ideological atmosphere among Ukrainian nationalist partisans during the same period as Babi Yar — distinct from OUN-B/UPA (Borovets later clashed with Bandera), but showing the prevalence of antisemitic framing in wartime Ukrainian nationalist publications.

## PART VI: KEY PUBLISHED COMPILATIONS AND SECONDARY SOURCES

### 6.0 — Berkhoff, *Motherland in Danger: Soviet Propaganda During World War II* (2012)

Karel Berkhoff's monograph documents the Soviet propaganda machine's treatment of the Jewish massacres — including the suppression of their Jewish character. Key findings on Babi Yar:

**Soviet media acknowledgment of the massacre:**
- November 19, 1941: Citing TASS in New York, both Pravda and Izvestiia reported "the Germans executed in Kiev **52,000 Jews** — men, women, and children." (p. 150)
- November 29, 1941: Pravda referred to a "pogrom" in Kiev killing 52,000 (p. 150)
- November 1943 (Kiev recapture): *Krasnaia zvezda* identified murdered victims as "Jews, Communists, and the workers of a range of Soviet establishments"; *Izvestiia* quoted eyewitness Dmitrii Orlov, who "began by mentioning the Jews and then supposedly shifted his focus to 'the people.'" (p. 158)

**Von Reichenau order and Babi Yar:**
Berkhoff provides crucial context on how Stalin obtained the Reichenau order:
> Soviet officers discovered the instructions in the recaptured town of Kalinin (now Tver), and on January 14 [1942] the NKVD leader Lavrentii Beria sent Stalin and Molotov a photocopy and a generally accurate Russian translation. (p. 151)

The order was circulated among German troops specifically to suppress their unease about the Babi Yar massacre:
> Among other things, the Nazi general meant to suppress concerns among the German military about the mass murder of the Jews, such as that committed recently at Kiev's Babi Yar. (p. 151)

Stalin published the order in Pravda on January 15, 1942 — but with **all anti-Semitic passages removed** except "Asiatic-Jewish danger," making the mass murder of Jews invisible to Soviet readers (p. 151–152).

**Soviet suppression of the Jewish victims:**
The Soviet Extraordinary State Commission report on Nazi crimes in Kiev (December 1943–February 1944) deliberately replaced "Jews" with "citizens." Berkhoff quotes the final version, approved by Khrushchev and other officials:
> "On September 29, 1941, the Hitlerite bandits chased thousands of peaceful Soviet citizens to the corner of Melnik [sic] and Dokterev [sic] Streets. They brought them to Babi Yar, took away all their valuables, and then shot them." (p. 160)

## PART VIII: KORÜCK 585 WAR DIARY FINDINGS (RH 23/330)

### 8.1 — RH 23/330: Activity Report, June 18, 1941 – January 5, 1942

**Bundesarchiv-Militärarchiv**: RH 23/330  
**Document type**: "Urschrift. GEHEIM Nr. 148/42." — Secret activity report ("Darstellung der Tätigkeit sowie wichtiger Ereignisse und Maßnahmen")  
**Cover page**: Kommandant rückwärtiges Armeegebiet 585; Begonnen: 18.VI.1941, Abgeschlossen: 5.I.1942  
**Pages**: 87 total  
**Bundesarchiv UUID**: `efd22aef-f024-4733-af28-aa72bcd338f2`

This is the Korück 585 activity summary for the Barbarossa campaign through early 1942, covering the entirety of the Babi Yar period. Korück 585 subordinated to AOK 6 (6th Army, commanded by Field Marshal von Reichenau) "im Berichtszeitraum."

**KEY FINDINGS:**

#### 8.1.1 — Korück 585 as Stadtkommandant of Kiev (page 34)

The document explicitly records that Korück 585 served as **Stadtkommandant (military city commander) of Kiev** during September-October 1941:

> *"Von [mid-September] bis [mid-October/early November] 41 war Korück Stadtkommandant von Kiew."*

The report notes that upon Korück's arrival in Kiev, German soldiers had plundered civilian food stocks, necessitating intervention. An incident involving a fire at a warehouse on the Hauptstraße is described. The document then states:

> *"Von Ende Oktober bis Anfang November 41 war Korück als Stadtkommandant... im Charkow eingesetzt."*

**Significance:** As Stadtkommandant, Korück 585 was the highest military authority in Kiev during the period of the Babi Yar massacre (September 29–30, 1941). The 6th Army rear-area command was the occupying administrative authority responsible for the city when 33,771 Jews were killed.

Screenshot: `Koruck585_RH23_330_p34_Stadtkommandant_Kiew.jpg`

#### 8.1.2 — Reference to September 28, 1941 Orders (page 40)

In the supply/provisions section (signed by Dr. P.O.P.P., Intendanturrat), the document references two orders directly dated to the eve of Babi Yar:

> *"Der Befehl des Herrn Heeresmarschalls betr. Maßnahmen gegen [Requisitionierung] von Kiew und Landesbesitze beginnend am **28.9.41**, abgedruckt im HV Blatt 1941 C Nr. 771, und die Befehle des Oberbefehlshabers der 6. Armee v. **28.9.41** und 12.11.41 sind erneuert zum Gegenstand der Truppenbelehrung zu machen."*

**Translation:**
> "The Field Marshal's order regarding measures against [requisitioning/plundering] of Kiev and country property, dated **September 28, 1941**, published in HV Blatt 1941 C Nr. 771, and the orders of the Commander-in-Chief of the 6th Army dated **September 28, 1941** and November 12, 1941, are to be renewed as the subject of troop instruction."

**Significance:** September 28, 1941 is the day the **Bekanntmachung** (massacre-ordering poster) was posted across Kiev ordering Jews to report at Melnik/Doktorivska Streets on the morning of September 29. The existence of a Field Marshal's order dated the same day — published army-wide in the HV Blatt — and 6th Army orders from the same date confirms that high-level military orders accompanied and authorized the action. The reference to AOK 6 orders of September 28, 1941 is direct corroboration of Reichenau's command involvement in the events of Babi Yar.

Screenshot: `Koruck585_RH23_330_p40_Order28Sep1941.jpg`

#### 8.1.3 — POW Collection Operations in Kiev (page 34)

> *"Als am 20.VIII.41 abends von A.O.K. 6 der Befehl kam, dass sofort für 4.000 Ukrainier, die aus dem Feindgebiet im Kiew in Abmarsch waren, gesorgt werden musste, wurde diese Aufgabe dem Intendanten übertragen... Über diesen Einsatz wurde am 7.IX.41 berichtet."*

**Translation:** "On the evening of August 20, 1941, when the order came from AOK 6 that provision must immediately be made for 4,000 Ukrainians who were marching from enemy territory toward Kiev, this task was assigned to the quartermaster... A report on this deployment was filed on September 7, 1941."

#### 8.1.4 — Kharkov Concentration Camp Preparation (page 36)

The document also records that Korück's supply department (Abtlg. IVa) was tasked with preparations for a "Konzentrationslager" (concentration camp) planned in Kharkov, which "scheiterte schließlich am Problem der Beheizung und Bewachung" (ultimately failed due to problems of heating and guarding). A collection camp for 30,000 Russian workers was also organized.

#### 8.1.5 — Scale of POW Operations in Kiev Area (page 32)

The POW affairs section (internal page 12) documents the scale of military operations surrounding Kiev:

> *"In einer Auffangungssammelstelle waren allein nach und nach in 6 verschiedenen Abständen 107.000 Kriegsgefangene zu sammeln..."*

**Translation:** "At a single collection point alone, in 6 successive waves, 107,000 prisoners of war were assembled..."

**Significance:** This confirms that Korück 585 was managing one of the largest POW processing operations in military history simultaneously with the Babi Yar massacre. The Kiev encirclement (September 15–26, 1941) produced approximately 665,000 total POWs. The logistical apparatus handling this — including Korück 585's collection points — was operational precisely during the September 29–30 massacre.

Screenshot: `Koruck585_RH23_330_p32_107000POWs_Kriegsgefangenenwesen.jpg`

#### 8.1.6 — HV Blatt 771/41 as the Foundation of Kiev's Military Administration (page 30)

In the Stadtkommandant narrative section (internal page 11), the document confirms the September 28 Field Marshal's order governed all aspects of civilian supply in Kiev:

> *"Verantwortlich für die Ernährung der Zivilbevölkerung waren gemäß O.B.Bef. vom .../.41 (HV Bl. 771/41) jedoch die Wirtschaftsfeststellungen in Zusammenarbeit mit der zivilen Stadtverwaltung..."*

> *"Während der 9 Wochen, wo Korück gleichzeitig Stadt-Kommandant war, hatte Abtlg. IVa an laufenden Bearbeitungen herangezogen. Als die Fragen der Ernährung von 600.000 Einwohnern der total ausgebombten Stadt betrafen."*

**Translation:**
> "Responsibility for feeding the civilian population rested, according to the Commander-in-Chief's order .../.41 (HV Bl. 771/41), with the Economic Survey agencies in cooperation with the civilian city administration..."
>
> "During the 9 weeks in which Korück simultaneously served as Stadtkommandant, Department IVa was drawn into ongoing administrative work regarding the feeding of 600,000 inhabitants of the totally bombed-out city."

**Significance:** This second reference to HV Bl. 771/41 — now explicitly tied to the Stadtkommandant function and the 600,000-person civilian feeding problem — confirms the September 28, 1941 Field Marshal's order was the foundational administrative instrument for Korück's entire governance of Kiev. The "9 Wochen" (9 weeks) as Stadtkommandant runs from approximately mid-September to mid-November 1941, precisely encompassing Babi Yar (Sept 29–30).

Screenshot: `Koruck585_RH23_330_p30_HVBlatt771_Stadtkommandant_Ernaehrung.jpg`

#### 8.1.6 — Jews Explicitly Listed as Forced Labor under Korück 585 (page 47, Annex 5)

**Date of document**: August 30, 1941 — exactly one month before Babi Yar.

Annex 5 to the Korück 585 Kommandeur-Befehl (Commander's Order) instructs all subordinate Ortskommandanturen in the rear area to collect textile goods ("Spinnstoffe") from abandoned buildings. The order specifies the labor to be used:

> **"Sämtliche Kommandanturen in rückw. Armeegebiet haben sich deshalb für diese Sammlung zu interessieren und durch Sammelkommandos (Juden, Kriegsgefangene, not - falls auch Ukrainer) diese Stoffe sammeln zu lassen..."**

**Translation:**
> "All commandant offices in the rear army area are therefore to concern themselves with this collection and have the goods collected by collection detachments **(Jews, prisoners of war, if necessary also Ukrainians)**..."

**Significance:** This order, signed by Department IVa of Korück 585 and dated August 30, 1941, is direct documentary evidence that **Jews within the Korück 585 operational area were being used as a controlled, categorized labor force under military authority one month before Babi Yar**. Jews are listed first in the hierarchy of forced labor — before POWs and Ukrainians. This places Jews firmly within the administrative machinery of Korück 585 well before the September 29–30 massacre.

Screenshot: `Koruck585_RH23_330_p47_Juden_Sammelkommando_30Aug1941.jpg`

#### 8.1.7 — Jewish "Evacuees" under Korück Supervision, Transferred to Civilian Authority September 6, 1941 (pages 49–50, Annexes)

The Tätigkeitsbericht for the Radomysl sub-commandant (Annex 6) records that in early September 1941, approximately 6,000–10,000 Jewish "Evaluierten" (evacuees — Nazi bureaucratic terminology for Jews being forcibly relocated) were held in camps in the Berdichev/Radomysl area under Korück 585 supervision:

> *[Page 49, 28.8.41 entry]:* "...es war notwendig, daß sofort für 6.000 Evaluierten, die aus dem Feindgebiet... sofort Unterkunft und Verpflegung zu stellen waren..."  

> *[Page 50, 5.9.41 entry]:* "...ca. Zehntausend Juden, Frauen und Männer... Die Küche war in baulichen Zustand. Die Zubereitung der Nahrungsmittel durch einige Männer und Frauen aus dem Kreis der Evaluierten...  Zusammenfassend kann gesagt werden, daß die Versorgung der Evaluierten sehr schlecht und unzulänglich ist."

> *[Page 50, 6.9.41 entry]:* **"Eine neue Situation ist am 6.9. durch die plötzliche Entscheidung der Zivilverwaltung (Gauleiter Koch) eingetreten. Dem Evaluiertenlager wird gereist nur durch den Stadtkommissar bereichert durch die Zivilverwaltung. Es erscheint also unbestritten, daß diese Aktion der Zivilverwaltung dem Militär entzogen ist."**

**Translation:**
> [28.8.41]: "...it was necessary to immediately provide accommodation and food for 6,000 evacuees who [came] from enemy territory..."
>
> [5.9.41]: "...approximately ten thousand Jews, women and men... The kitchen was in a dilapidated state. Food preparation was done by some men and women from among the evacuees... In summary, the supply of the evacuees is very poor and inadequate."
>
> [6.9.41]: **"A new situation arose on September 6 through the sudden decision of the civilian administration (Gauleiter Koch). The evacuee camp will henceforth be accessible only through the town commissioner [Stadtkommissar] appointed by the civilian administration. It thus appears clear that this action has been withdrawn from military [jurisdiction]."**

**Significance:** This document records a critical jurisdictional transfer: Korück 585 military authority over approximately 10,000 Jews in the Berdichev area was abruptly replaced by Gauleiter Erich Koch's Reich Commissariat Ukraine (RKU) civilian administration on September 6, 1941 — 23 days before Babi Yar. The transfer of Jews from military to civilian/SS-police jurisdiction was the standard procedural step that preceded organized mass shootings in the occupied Soviet Union. The Babi Yar massacre was organized jointly by SS-Gruppenführer Friedrich Jeckeln (Higher SS and Police Leader) and AOK 6, both of which operated independently of — but in parallel to — Koch's RKU.
