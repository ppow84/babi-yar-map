### Dean's Key Finding
The main mass shooting of September 29–30, 1941 occurred in the **western spur** of the Babyn Yar ravine — not the southern section as some earlier researchers proposed. Dean identified the route using:
- Luftwaffe aerial photos from NARA (Sept 26, 1943)
- Hähle ground photographs (Sept/Oct 1941)
- "The lone tree atop the crescent-shaped cliff" as a geographic anchor
- Cross-referencing with witness testimony (Pronicheva, Yekel, others)
- 3D terrain reconstruction by Maks Rokmaniko / Spatial Tech team

## BYHMC Hähle Route Analysis PDF (BYHMC_Haehle_route_analysis.pdf)

**Author:** Dr. Martin Dean, Historical Researcher at the Institute of History and Memory Landscape of Babyn Yar at the BYHMC

**Title:** "The route taken by Johannes Haehle when he photographed the aftermath of the mass shooting at Babyn Yar"

**Pages:** 13. Downloaded from babynyar.org.

**Aerial source used:** NARA, GX 3938, No. 104, September 26, 1943.

### Key content by page:
- **p.1**: Introduction. Malakov (2004) identified Kyiv city locations but not ravine locations. Dean uses aerial + ground + 3D to reconstruct Hähle's route.
- **p.2**: Hähle #008-004 (clothing pile) — line of sight plotted on aerial. Hähle near southern entrance to sand quarry, camera pointing W of due N.
- **p.3**: 3D projection of #008-004 onto terrain model (courtesy Rokmaniko/BYHMC). Clothing piles curve left indicating undressing path. Testimony of Albin Powa (Police Bn 45) and Erich Karrasch (Police Bn 303).
- **pp.4–5**: Hähle #008-005 (massive clothing pile, cliff wall). Line of sight on aerial. Enlargement showing Waffen-SS rummaging for valuables.
- **p.6**: Hähle #008-006 (three officers atop cliff). Taken along western perimeter of sand quarry near the cut entrance. Fritz Forberg (Police Bn 45) testimony.
- **p.7**: Officers on "Field Marshall's Hill" (*Feldherrnhügel*). Viktor Trill (Sk 4a driver) testimony — Blobel, Callsen, Radetzky present on plateau.
- **pp.8–9**: Hähle #008-007 (close-up clothing). Then #008-014 — Hähle ascends to plateau overlooking western spur. Photo taken from roughly half-way along ravine. Waffen-SS soldier guarding POW work (3rd Company, Waffen SS Bn zbV, attached to Sk 4a). POW N.B. Sokolov testimony.
- **p.10**: **KEY IMAGE** — Annotated aerial with numbered triangles (#4, #5, #6, #14, #15, #16) showing Hähle's camera positions and lines of sight for each photo. Complete route reconstruction through the sand quarry and western spur.
- **pp.10–11**: Hähle #008-015 — the most iconic image, looking straight down the western spur with the crescent-shaped cliff. Viktor Trill testimony (Sept 30, second day). "Lone tree" on cliff edge identified on both ground and aerial photos.
- **p.12**: Enlargement of #008-015 showing lone tree. Hähle #008-016 — last photo in the Babyn Yar series, discolorations in sand from explosions used to bury corpses.
- **p.13**: Conclusion + footnotes. Acknowledgments: Hrynevich, Rukkas, Berkhoff, Umansky, Godziemba-Maliszewski, Kruglov, Kalnytsky, Pasternak, Doroshenko, Rohmaniyko, Kavatsiuk.
